Wedding Budget Tips for Nigeria

Nigerian weddings are joyful celebrations, but costs can rise quickly without proper planning. These tips help you spend wisely while still enjoying your big day.

  • Limit guest count: More guests mean higher food, drinks, seating, and logistics costs.
  • Choose your city carefully: Lagos and Abuja weddings cost significantly more.
  • Combine ceremonies: Hosting traditional and white weddings on the same day reduces expenses.
  • Rent instead of buying: Attire and décor can often be rented at lower cost.
  • Negotiate vendors: Negotiation is normal and expected in Nigeria.
  • Set a firm budget: Decide your maximum spend before booking any vendor.

Frequently Asked Questions About Wedding Costs in Nigeria

How much does a wedding cost in Nigeria?

Wedding costs vary by guest count, city, and wedding type. Small weddings may cost under ₦1 million, while large weddings in Lagos or Abuja can exceed ₦10 million.

Is a traditional wedding cheaper than a white wedding?

Traditional weddings are often cheaper, but aso-ebi, live bands, and large guest lists can significantly increase costs.

Why are Lagos weddings expensive?

Lagos weddings involve premium venues, higher vendor fees, logistics challenges, and higher guest expectations.

Can I plan a good wedding on a low budget?

Yes. By limiting guests, choosing affordable venues, and negotiating vendors, many couples plan beautiful weddings on modest budgets.

How accurate are the estimates?

Estimates are based on real Nigerian market ranges. Actual costs depend on vendors, negotiation, inflation, and personal preferences.
